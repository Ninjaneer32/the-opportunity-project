About the Training

Start Date: October 16, 2019

Duration: 7 weeks

  • This training is facilitated by HCD experts from the Census Open Innovation Labs.
  • The program begins October 16-18, 2019 with a 3-day intensive which includes instruction, hands on applications, and site visits to some of the most innovative workplaces around the DC area.
  • Over the following 7 weeks, small teams will develop and implement solutions to the question: How might we re-imagine the employee experience to support innovation at the Census Bureau?
  • Each small team will be assigned an HCD mentor and join at least 2 of 3 coaching sessions.
  • Upon completiong of the program, participants will receive a Certificate of Completion, receive 22.5 hours of supervisor training credits, and may be invited to present their projects to hundreds of stakeholders and media at a Demo Day event on December 10, 2019.
  • There is no fee associated with this training.

Training Requirements

To receive a certificate of completion, participants must:

  • Attend the October 16-18, 2019 training, including site visits (no exceptions)
  • Attend at least 2 out of 3 in-person coaching session on 10/30, 11/13, and 12/4
  • Complete a small group Human-Centered Design project between October 21 - December 9, 2019 (should require no more than 3-4 hours per week)

Who Should Apply?

Full-time, HQ-based Census Bureau exmployees with supervisory status.

People who are excited about this opportunity who are:

  • Interested in and want to learn more about HCD
  • Change makers who are innovation-oriented
  • Open to iteration, experimentation and feedback
  • Aspiring or proven leaders who will help champion HCD

How to Apply

Email all application materials in one email to census.HCDtraining@census.gov by 11:59pm ET Wednesday September 18, 2019.

Your application has 3 components:

  1. A brief application
  2. An email from your direct supervisor approving your participation in the full training if accepted
  3. A nomination email or letter from a supervisor or someone in a position senior to you
